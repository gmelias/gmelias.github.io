John Wooden is a Legend.

Here is his story:

John Wooden was a both a college backetball player and coach, but he is most well-known for his time at UCLA. There, he won 10 NCAA championships, including 7 in a row (which was a record). He became widely known for his Pyramid of Success. This not only gave guidance on how to be a better athlete, but on how to be successful in life. He cared about his athletes both on and off the court, and encouraged them to work hard in the classroom.

About the Pyramid of Success
John Wooden spent a large portion of his life studying successful people and the traits that led them there. These traits were not limited to basketball or athletics. By the time he was 38, he had narrowed the list down to 25, which is displayed in a triangle fashion. Some examples of these traits include:

  • Industriousness - There is no substitute for work. Worthwhile results come from hard work and careful planning.
  • Initiative - Cultivate the ability to make decisions and think alone. Do not be afraid of failure, but learn from it.
  • Skill - A knowledge of and the ability to properly and quickly execute the fundamentals. Be prepared and cover every little detail.
